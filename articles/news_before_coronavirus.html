Major News Sources Unsure of what They Talked About Before Coronavirus

March 15, 2020

With the never ending stream of recent news, it would seem that the world's news sources would be doing well. "But they're not," CNN CEO Jeff Zucker explained to us. "Frankly we're having an internal crisis - we're not sure what we used to talk about before coronavirus came about. We're not sure how we're going to continue publishing content after it's over."

Zucker is not alone, either. CEO of Fox News, Roger Ailes, explained to us their internal struggle to dig up archived content in order to review it.

"Obviously there's no shortage of material right now." Ailes told us. "But we're just not sure how long that's going to last, and we're afraid that we've lost our ability to make nothing sound like something. It's getting to the point where we're kind of hoping that this virus continues for a while. Sorry, that's off the record, right?"

"I hope we're not missing anything important." Zucker continued. "We used to be able to report everyday without coronavirus. Now it's all we report on. What used to happen?"
